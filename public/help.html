OpenNono

Description

OpenNono is an open-source JavaScript port of Nono o Sagase! Level 41! ~Nono to Fushigi na Ehon~ , a Minesweeper maze game. The goal of the game is to navigate the player's idols, or whatever remains of them, from the entry of the map to the goal on the right edge, without stepping on any of the hidden mines.

Controls

  • Arrow keys
    Moves the cursor. When the flagging mode is off, this also moves the active idol and then reveals the tile beneath the idol.
  • Left mouse click
    While in flagging mode, places or removes a flag at the cursor tile; otherwise, moves the active idol to the tile under the mouse cursor, and reveals that tile. The idol cannot move to unrevealed tiles that are not adjacent to any revealed tiles.
  • F key
    Toggles flagging mode on or off.
  • Space key
    While in flagging mode, places or removes a flag at the cursor tile. While using an Open skill, rotates the shape of the tiles to be revealed.
  • 1 / 2 / 3 / 4 / 5 key
    Selects one of the idols. Dead idols cannot be selected as an active idol. Also used to select the skill target.
  • S key
    Uses the active idol's skill, or cancels the skill being used.
  • N key
    After reaching the goal, proceeds to the next stage.

Skills

Each idol can equip a skill that aids the player during the game. Every skill is associated with a cool time; using a skill resets its charge to the cool time, and the skill cannot be used again until its charge is zero. Revealing a tile reduces the charge of all skills on living idols by 1, and all idols can use their skills at the beginning of every level.

The different skill types are as follows:

Type Cool time Target Description
None N/A None Does nothing. Cannot be used.
Open 5 / 10 / 14 / 18 / 22 N/A Reveals 1 to 5 tiles within a given shape oriented along a chosen direction relative to the idol. Mines on these tiles are revealed without killing the idol. The cool time is higher for shapes that contain more tiles.
Heal 28 Any dead idol Revives a dead idol.
Guard 40 Current idol, if this skill is not activated Allows the idol to step on a mine without dying, once. The skill loses its effect if the player switches to another idol.
Quick 4 / 5 / 6 / 7 Any living idol with a skill not fully charged Reduces the charge of the targeted idol's skill by (2 + the cool time of this skill).
Special 55 Current idol Randomly converts 2 tiles into Tomodachi Blocks. At most one idol may equip this skill.

Similar titles

  • Minehunt by Hewlett-Packard
    A Minesweeper maze game for the HP 48 series graphing calculators. The map dimension is fixed at 16 × 8 and contains 16 randomly placed mines. Tiles do not flood-fill surrounding tiles when revealed, and only the clue for the player's current tile is available at any time. It is possible that the starting position is entirely surrounded by mines.
    • SNOWEDGE by Andrew Gremlich
      Web clone of the above calculator game. A text terminal Rust port from the same author appears to be in the works.
    • Maze Frontier by MAGIC SEVEN
      An Android / iOS maze game with Minesweeper elements. The player has 4 lives and can cross through diagonally adjacent mines. Flood-filling is limited to the 8 immediately surrounding tiles around the player. Story maps have entirely deterministic layouts; the game's endless mode generates random Minesweeper levels, and guarantees there are no mines near the starting position.
    • Maze-Minesweeper by Antephus, AllyT94, jblomme, and LoPCY7
      Very incomplete, but it shows up as one of the top search results for "minesweeper maze".
  • Nono o Sagase! Level 41! ~Nono to Fushigi na Ehon~ by Cygames / BANDAI NAMCO Entertainment
    The original source of this port, available as the 2019 April Fools' Day exclusive minigame of THE IDOLM@STER Cinderella Girls: Starlight Stage. The main differences are that it is now possible to use 5 Open skills of the same kind, even for shapes which were owned by fewer than 5 idols from the original minigame; and that all Tomodachi Blocks now reveal 3 random mines (i.e. stage 2 / 3 variants are not implemented).
  • Minesweeper Maze by Dan Liu
    An iOS application no longer available from the Apple App Store. The screenshots suggest that player movement is separate from tile revealing, and that additional walls on tile boundaries can be revealed that constrain player movement.
